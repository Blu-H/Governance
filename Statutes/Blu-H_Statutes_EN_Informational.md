## I. Name, Purpose, and Registered Office

### Article 1 — Name

An association without profit motive is hereby established under the name  
**“Blu-H”**, abbreviated **“Blu-H”** (hereinafter referred to as *the Association*).

---

### Article 2 — Purpose

The purpose of the Association is to **increase the impact, coherence, and visibility
of efforts aimed at mitigating risks related to floods, droughts, and other
environmental hazards, including wildfires**.

The Association seeks to **strengthen the resilience of vulnerable communities** at
national and international levels, including in support of the humanitarian sector.

To achieve its purpose, the Association may in particular undertake the following
activities:

#### Core Areas of Action

- **Expert consultation**  
  Providing strategic review, management support, and coordination assistance to
  public and humanitarian partners, improving the quality and effectiveness of
  disaster risk reduction initiatives.

- **Capacity building and training**  
  Developing and delivering training sessions and specialised workshops enabling
  professionals and communities to anticipate, prepare for, and respond to crises.

- **Innovative operational services**  
  Developing, activating, and deploying advanced tools and services that provide
  early warning systems and rapid, data-driven support during humanitarian
  emergencies and environmental risk events.

The Association may also collaborate with:
- Public and governmental entities;
- Universities and research institutions;
- Humanitarian NGOs;
- Intergovernmental organisations, including the United Nations;
- Private-sector partners.

---

### Article 3 — Independence

The Association operates in **strict political, ideological, and religious
independence**.

---

### Article 4 — Registered Office

The registered office of the Association is located in the **municipality of Kayl,
Luxembourg**.

The registered office may be transferred to another address within the same
municipality by decision of the Board of Directors.

---

### Article 5 — Duration

The duration of the Association is **unlimited**.

---

## II. Members

### Article 6 — Admission

The Association shall have **at least two members**.

Members are admitted by decision of the **Board of Directors** following a written
application. Admission decisions are taken by **absolute majority** of directors
present or represented. The Board is not required to justify refusal of admission.

---

### Article 7 — Membership Fees

Members shall pay an **annual membership fee**, the amount of which is determined by
the General Assembly and may not exceed **EUR 1,000**.

Any contribution exceeding the annual fee shall be considered a **voluntary donation**
made to the Association to support its purpose.

The Board of Directors may grant partial or total exemptions from membership fees
under certain conditions.

---

### Article 8 — Obligations of Members

Members undertake to respect:
- The purpose and principles of the Association;
- Decisions of the General Assembly and the Board of Directors.

Any application for membership implies full acceptance of these Articles of
Association.

---

### Article 9 — Resignation

Members may resign at any time by submitting written notice to the Board of
Directors.

Any member who has not paid the required membership fee within three months of the
due date shall be deemed to have resigned.

Membership terminates automatically upon the death of a member.

---

### Article 10 — Exclusion

Members may be excluded if they seriously prejudice the interests of the Association
or fail to comply with these Articles.

From the time an exclusion proposal is made by the Board of Directors until the final
decision of the General Assembly (by a two-thirds majority of votes), the member
concerned shall be **automatically suspended** from all functions.

---

### Article 11 — Register of Members

The Association maintains a **register of members** at its registered office under
the responsibility of the Board of Directors.

The register records:
- For natural persons: name, surname, and address;
- For legal entities: name, legal form, registered office, registration number, and
  register name.

All decisions relating to admission, resignation, or exclusion must be recorded
within one month.

Departing members have **no claim on the Association’s assets** and may not request
reimbursement of paid contributions.

---

### Article 12 — Right of Inspection

Any member may consult or request a copy of:
- The register of members;
- Minutes and decisions of the General Assembly and Board of Directors;
- Accounting documents;
- The consolidated Articles of Association.

Documents may not be removed from the registered office.

---

## III. Board of Directors

### Article 13 — Composition

The Association is administered by a **Board of Directors composed of at least three
directors**, elected from among the members by the General Assembly.

The term of office is **three years**, renewable.

---

### Article 14 — Officers

The Board of Directors acts collegially and may appoint from among its members a
**President, a Secretary, and a Treasurer**.

---

### Article 15 — Meetings and Decisions

- Meetings are convened at least **eight days in advance**;
- Directors may grant a written proxy to another director (one proxy per person);
- The Board may deliberate validly if at least **half of the directors** are present
  or represented;
- Decisions are taken by **absolute majority**;
- Participation by videoconference or other identifiable remote means is permitted
  and deemed equivalent to physical presence.

---

### Article 16 — Powers

The Board of Directors is vested with the **broadest powers** for administration and
management of the Association, except for matters expressly reserved to the General
Assembly by law or these Articles.

The Association is validly represented and bound by the **joint signature of two
directors**.

---

### Article 17 — Daily Management

The Board of Directors may delegate the **daily management** of the Association to
one or more persons, subject to prior approval by the General Assembly.

The delegate is appointed for a **six-year term** and is accountable annually to the
General Assembly for any remuneration or benefits.

---

### Article 18 — Reporting Obligations

Within six months following the end of each financial year, the Board of Directors
shall submit to the General Assembly:
- An activity report;
- The annual accounts;
- The budget for the following financial year;
- Where applicable, the appointment of an approved auditor.

---

## IV. General Assembly

Articles governing the General Assembly define:
- Annual and extraordinary meetings;
- Matters reserved to the General Assembly (including statutes, board appointments,
  accounts, dissolution, and public-interest status);
- Equal voting rights (one member, one vote);
- The possibility of participation by remote means.

---

## V. Dissolution and Liquidation

In the event of dissolution, the remaining assets of the Association shall be
allocated, after settlement of liabilities, to:
- Another non-profit association or public-interest foundation established in an
  EU or EEA Member State;
- A social impact company whose capital consists entirely of impact shares;
- The State, a municipality, or a public body.

---

## VI. Amendments to the Articles

Any amendment to these Articles must be adopted by the General Assembly in accordance
with the quorum and majority requirements laid down by Luxembourg law and published
in RESA to take effect.

---

## VII. Financial Year

The financial year of the Association runs from **1 January to 31 December**.

---

## VIII. Final Provisions

For all matters not governed by these Articles, the **Law of 7 August 2023 on
non-profit associations and foundations**, as well as the Association’s internal
regulations, shall apply.
