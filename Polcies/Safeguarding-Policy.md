
# Safeguarding Policy (Public Version)

**Organisation:** Blu-H ASBL (Luxembourg)  
**Status:** Draft  
**Applies to:** Blu-H ASBL  
**Owner:** Board of Directors  
**Last updated:** 20 December 2025  
**Next review:** 30 June 2026  

---

## 1. Purpose

Blu-H is committed to ensuring that its activities do not cause harm and that people
engaged with or affected by its work are treated with dignity, respect, and care.

This Safeguarding Policy sets out Blu-H’s principles and approach to preventing harm,
exploitation, abuse, or inappropriate conduct in the context of humanitarian,
scientific, and public-interest activities, including disaster-related contexts.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- Members of Blu-H  
- Board members and advisors  
- Volunteers, citizen helpers, and contributors  
- Partners and collaborators acting on behalf of Blu-H  
- Individuals and communities affected by disasters or crisis events  

Blu-H’s work may involve challenging international and humanitarian contexts, including
disaster-affected regions and vulnerable populations. Safeguarding principles apply in
all such contexts, including remote, digital, and data-driven engagement.

---

## 3. Safeguarding Principles

Blu-H is guided by the following principles:

- **Do no harm**  
- **Respect and dignity**  
- **Protection of vulnerable people**  
- **Proportionality**  
- **Accountability**  

---

## 4. Protection from Sexual Exploitation, Abuse and Harassment (PSEAH)

Blu-H has **zero tolerance** for sexual exploitation and abuse (SEA) and sexual
harassment (SH) linked to its work.

---

## 5. Behaviour Standards (Minimum)

All individuals acting on behalf of Blu-H must:

- Never engage in sexual exploitation or abuse  
- Never exchange assistance or access for sex  
- Never engage in sexual activity with anyone under 18, regardless of local law  
- Never abuse position, authority, or access  
- Report safeguarding concerns promptly  

---

## 6. Citizen Helpers and Volunteers

Blu-H commits to avoiding placing volunteers or citizen helpers in unsafe situations and
ensuring participation is voluntary, informed, and ethical.

---

## 7. Disaster-Affected Contexts

Blu-H prioritises safety, avoids publishing harmful information, and does not deploy
individuals into disaster zones or replace emergency responders.

---

## 8. Reporting Concerns

Safeguarding concerns can be reported confidentially to:

- **Email:** info@blu-h.org  

No retaliation will occur for good-faith reporting.

---

## 9. Survivor-Centred Response

Blu-H prioritises safety, confidentiality, dignity, and choice in responding to
safeguarding concerns.

---

## 10. Response and Escalation

Blu-H will assess concerns promptly and cooperate with partners, authorities, or other
relevant bodies as required by law or to protect people from harm.

---

## 11. Data and Safeguarding

Safeguarding is closely linked to responsible data use. Further detail is provided in
Blu-H’s **Data, Privacy & Ethics Statement**.

---

## 12. Review

This policy will be reviewed periodically and updated as Blu-H’s activities evolve.

---

## References and Alignment

This policy is informed by and aligned with recognised international standards,
including:

- UN Inter-Agency Standing Committee (IASC) – Six Core Principles  
- UN Protection from Sexual Exploitation and Abuse (PSEA) Frameworks  
- Core Humanitarian Standard (CHS) on Quality and Accountability  
- EU DG ECHO safeguarding and PSEA expectations
